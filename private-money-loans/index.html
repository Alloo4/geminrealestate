The Basics

What is private money lending?

A private money loan is real estate financing from a private lender — an individual or fund — rather than a bank. Because terms are negotiated deal by deal, private money moves faster and flexes further than conventional financing: it’s underwritten on the deal and the equity, not just your W-2.

That speed is the point. Fix & flip acquisitions, gap shortfalls, and bridge situations run on contract deadlines that banks can’t hit. The trade-off is that every private lender has their own appetite — positions they’ll take, projects they’ll fund, markets they know. Matching the deal to the right lender is the whole game, and it’s the part GIREC does for you.
